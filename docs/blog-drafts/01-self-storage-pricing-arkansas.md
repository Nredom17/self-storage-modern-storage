### Heading (H2)

The climate-controlled premium — when it's worth it

### Paragraph

Climate-controlled units in Arkansas run about 25–50% more per month than equivalent drive-up units. For sensitive items — furniture, electronics, photos, mattresses, instruments, business inventory — the premium is usually worth it. Arkansas summer humidity routinely tops 70% and outdoor unit interiors hit 110°F+ in direct July sun. Those conditions accumulate damage on wood, leather, paper, and electronics the longer items sit. A single warped dresser, mildewed mattress, or fried TV exceeds a year of the climate-controlled premium. For tools, plastic bins, patio furniture, and durable items that already live in a hot garage, climate-controlled is overkill — standard drive-up is the more affordable choice.

### Heading (H2)

One-time fees you'll see at move-in

### Paragraph

Beyond monthly rent, three line items show up on your first bill at any storage operator, and they should be disclosed honestly on the reservation page before you check out. At Modern Storage® they are:

### Bullet list

- **One-time admin / setup fee** — covers account setup, lock and access-code provisioning, document processing. Often waived or reduced during move-in promotions. Exact current amount shows up on the reservation page before you check out — no surprise at the counter.
- **Insurance or tenant protection plan** — required. Many homeowners and renters insurance policies extend coverage to off-premises storage at no extra cost; bring proof at move-in and you may not need the facility plan. If you don't have qualifying coverage, the tenant protection plan scales with the declared value of your stored items (common tiers cover $2,000 / $3,000 / $5,000 / $10,000 of contents).
- **Disc lock or padlock** — bring one from home, or buy a disc lock at the facility office at move-in. Disc locks are recommended over standard padlocks because the shrouded shackle resists bolt cutters and weather.

### Heading (H2)

Fees that only apply in specific situations

### Paragraph

A few line items show up only when something specific happens during your rental. These are not standard charges — they exist because storage operators need to be made whole when a unit goes into default or comes back damaged.

### Bullet list

- **Late fee** — applies only if monthly rent is past due beyond the grace period in your rental agreement. The grace period and exact fee amount are stated in your lease.
- **Lien and auction fees** — apply only after multiple missed payments per Arkansas self-storage lien law. Modern Storage® makes multiple contact attempts before any unit reaches auction; this is the absolute last step, not a routine charge.
- **Damage to the unit** — physical damage beyond normal wear (drilled holes, oil-stained floor, broken door) can result in repair charges. Standard storage use — dings, dust, minor scuffs — does not.
- **Abandoned items disposal** — if items are left in the unit at closeout, a disposal fee covers removal. Donate, recycle, or take items with you instead.

Do storage rates go up after move-in?

### Paragraph

Storage rates can change over time based on market conditions, the same way an apartment lease renewal can. ## === FAQS (block editor — 4 entries) ===

### Q1

What's the cheapest storage unit in Arkansas?

### A1

The cheapest self-storage unit at most Arkansas locations is a 5x5 drive-up unit (25 sq ft, walk-in closet equivalent), typically $25–$45 per month before any move-in promotion. For roughly the same price as a small storage unit, a 5x10 (50 sq ft) drive-up usually runs $45–$75 — about $20–$30 more for double the floor space. If you're optimizing for cost per square foot, the 5x10 is often the better deal. Climate-controlled costs about 25–50% more across every size.

### Q2

Is the admin fee at Modern Storage® refundable?

### A2

The one-time admin fee at move-in covers account setup, lock and access-code provisioning, and document processing — it's not refundable because the setup work happens regardless of how long you rent. The fee is often waived or reduced during move-in promotions; the exact current amount shows on the reservation page before you complete the rental. Move-in promotions vary monthly, so check the reservation page or call your local facility for current offers.

### Q3

Do I have to use the storage facility's insurance, or can I use my own?

### A3

You can use your own homeowners or renters insurance if it extends to off-premises storage. Many policies do at no extra cost — check your policy or call your insurance provider. Bring proof of coverage at move-in and you may not need the facility's tenant protection plan at all. If your own policy doesn't extend, Modern Storage® offers a tenant protection plan that scales with the declared value of your stored items, with common tiers covering $2,000, $3,000, $5,000, or $10,000 of contents.

### Q4

How do I know if I'm getting a fair price compared to other storage operators?

### A4

Compare three things across operators: the standard rate (not the move-in promo), every fee disclosed up front, and the move-out terms. Modern Storage® publishes the typical price range for each size on the [pricing page](/pricing), every fee that can apply on a rental in a transparent table, and the honest no-move-out-fee, no-early-termination-fee policy. If a competitor is significantly cheaper on the headline rate but vague about admin fees, insurance requirements, or move-out terms, the all-in cost can end up higher. Honest pricing is the differentiator.
