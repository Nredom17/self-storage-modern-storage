## === INTRO ===

Picking a storage unit size is the single most common mistake renters make. Go too small and your stuff doesn't fit — you end up either renting a second unit or playing Tetris with your belongings. Go way too big and you're paying for empty floor space every month. This post walks through the four-step framework Modern Storage® uses internally to size customers right the first time, plus the price-by-size table so you can match the recommendation to your budget.

## === QUICK ANSWER ===

For a studio apartment, a 5x10 (50 sq ft). For a one-bedroom apartment, a 10x10 (100 sq ft) — the most popular size. For a two-bedroom apartment, a 10x15 (150 sq ft). For a three-bedroom home or one-car garage equivalent, a 10x20 (200 sq ft). For a four-to-five-bedroom home or business inventory, a 10x30 (300 sq ft). For closet overflow and seasonal items, a 5x5 (25 sq ft). When in doubt between two sizes, choose the larger — sizing down later is a simple in-facility transfer.

## === BODY ===

### Paragraph

The right storage unit size depends on what you're storing and how much breathing room you want. Most household movers pick from one of six sizes — 5x5, 5x10, 10x10, 10x15, 10x20, 10x30 — and the choice usually comes down to room equivalents. A 5x10 fits a studio. A 10x10 fits a one-bedroom. A 10x20 fits a three-bedroom home or a one-car garage of stuff. Here's the full framework.

### Heading (H2)

The four-step framework for picking a storage unit size

### Paragraph

The Modern Storage® team uses the same four steps to size customers in person. It works in about a minute if you know what you're moving:

### Numbered list

1. **Count the biggest items first.** Beds, sofas, dressers, refrigerators, washer/dryer, dining table. These set the minimum floor space — boxes and small items fit around them.
2. **Match those items to a base size.** Studio (a mattress + small sofa + boxes) = 5x10. One-bedroom (queen set + living room + appliance + boxes) = 10x10. Two-bedroom (multiple bed sets + dining + appliances) = 10x15. Three-bedroom or one-car garage (full home contents) = 10x20. Large home or business inventory = 10x30.
3. **Add a size up if you need walking aisle access.** If you'll be pulling items out mid-rental, you need a center aisle — that adds ~25% to your space need. If you'll pack wall-to-wall and not open the unit until move-out day, the base size is fine.
4. **Choose climate-controlled if you're storing sensitive items.** Furniture, electronics, photos, mattresses, instruments, business inventory, anything long-term — these belong in climate-controlled. Tools, plastic bins, patio furniture, durable garage overflow — drive-up is fine.

### Heading (H2)

When to size up vs size down

### Paragraph

The classic mistake is going one size too small to save $20-$40 a month. It costs more in the long run because the unit feels packed before you've even moved in, and you end up either renting a second unit at a separate facility or stuffing everything in with no aisle access. The classic over-rent is the opposite — paying for 200 sq ft when 100 was enough.

### Bullet list

- **Size up when:** you'll need to walk into the unit mid-rental to pull seasonal items, you have oversized furniture (sectionals, multiple beds, large dining tables), or you'd rather have breathing room than fill every cubic foot.
- **Size down when:** the items are highly stackable (sealed bins, identical boxes), you're storing flat-pack furniture, or you'll pack the unit once and not open it until move-out.
- **When in doubt:** size up. Modern Storage® rentals are month-to-month, and if you find you're using less space than you thought, the team can transfer you internally to a smaller unit at the same facility — no early-termination fee, only pay the new rate from the transfer date forward.

### Heading (H2)

How storage unit price scales with size

### Paragraph

Sizing up roughly doubles the price each step. A 5x10 isn't twice the price of a 5x5 (closer to 1.5×), but a 10x10 is about 2× a 5x10, and 10x20 is about 2× a 10x10. Climate-controlled adds about 25-50% on top of any size. Picking the right size matters financially because the gap between 5x10 and 10x10 in Arkansas is roughly $40-$70 a month — over a year that's $480-$840 of difference, and over the lifetime of a long-term rental it adds up.

## === FAQS ===

### Q1

What size storage unit fits a one-bedroom apartment?

### A1

A 10x10 storage unit (100 square feet) fits a typical one-bedroom apartment: a queen bedroom set with mattress and dresser, a living room set with couch and coffee table, a major appliance like a washer or refrigerator, 15-20 standard moving boxes, and a flat-screen TV. The 10x10 is the most popular size at Modern Storage® because it balances enough space for a full apartment with a price point most households can absorb. If you have oversized furniture (sectional sofa, multiple beds) or want walking aisle access, step up to a 10x15.

### Q2

What size storage unit fits a two-bedroom apartment?

### A2

A 10x15 storage unit (150 square feet) fits a typical two-bedroom apartment, including a queen mattress and frame, dresser, sofa, coffee table, dining set, TV, plus 20-30 boxes of personal items. If you have an oversized sectional, multiple beds, a large dining table, or you're storing for several months and want breathing room to walk in, step up to a 10x20 (200 square feet). A 10x10 is usually too small for a two-bedroom apartment unless you're storing only essential furniture and minimal boxes.

### Q3

What size storage unit do I need for a three-bedroom home?

### A3

A 10x20 storage unit (200 square feet, equivalent to a one-car garage) fits the contents of a three-bedroom home: full living, dining, and three bedroom sets, major appliances like refrigerator + washer + dryer, garage contents and tools, patio furniture, and up to 75 standard moving boxes. It's a common pick for three-bedroom home moves, whole-home renovations, and military PCS moves. For a four-to-five-bedroom home or significant business inventory, a 10x30 is the next size up.

### Q4

What if I pick the wrong storage unit size?

### A4

It happens often — and it's an easy fix at Modern Storage®. Rentals are month-to-month, so if a 5x10 turns out too small you can transfer to a 10x10 at the same facility without breaking a contract or paying an early-termination fee. The transfer is handled internally by the team — you pay the new rate from the transfer date forward. To size right the first time, use the AI Storage Size Finder on this site (~30 seconds), or call your local Modern Storage® facility and the team will help you pick the right size based on what you're actually moving.
